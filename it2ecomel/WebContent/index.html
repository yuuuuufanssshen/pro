Carbon Footprint

You produce a tiny amount of greenhouse gas when you breathe out. However, the things you do and buy produce large quantities.When you drive a car, the engine burns fuel which creates a certain amount of CO2, depending on its fuel consumption and the driving distance. The electricity you use at home was probably made by burning coal. The food you eat needed fertilizer to grow it and energy to harvest it. The clothes you wear, the toys you buy and the house you live in all took energy to make. Of course, we can't stop eating, playing or living in houses but there are things we can all do to reduce our carbon footprint.
